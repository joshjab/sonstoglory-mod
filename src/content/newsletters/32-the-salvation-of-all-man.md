# The Salvation of All Man

by Paul Jablonowski

July 31, 2018

<img src="pictures/dove_with_an_olive_branch.jpg" width="300" height="300" />

Noah released a dove which returned with a freshly plucked olive leaf in its beak, and therefore knew: *"that the waters had abated from the earth"* (Gen 8:11).

**The salvation of all man** is a doctrine, like the fresh olive branch the dove brought to Noah, which shows the flood of injustice and deception is coming to an end in our generation!

Understanding that God is able to save all of His people that He created is hard for most to comprehend because we are currently stuck in this thing called **time**. Therefore, we miss God's eternal nature and don't realize that He is able to finish that which He started. There is such injustice in our world (and the tension that comes with unresolved evil) that we desire judgment on things that are not right; But since God sees the end of **time**, He is much more merciful than we are. The truth is that all man will be restored and every single injustice will be resolved in due time.

Acts 3:21 says that Jesus will not return to earth from heaven until, *"the times of **restoration of all things** which God has spoken by the mouth of all His holy prophets since the world began."*

1 Corinthians 15:21-22 says: *"Since by man came death, by Man also came the resurrection of the dead. For **as in Adam ALL die**, even so **in Christ ALL shall be made alive.**"* This Scripture is very clear that ALL man have died through Adam, and likewise ALL man shall be saved in Jesus. The name Adam means "man." The Law of Jubilee, found in Leviticus 25, insures that all mankind will eventually be saved, but *"each in his own order"* as verse 23 says; which means that our choices in this lifetime will determine our position and authority in the new earth to come.

Death itself is the last enemy that will be destroyed (verse 26). The process we are currenlty living out "in time" is putting satan under Christ's feet! Death was defeated by Jesus "the firstfruit" 2,000 years ago. It will be defeated by the overcomers at "the first resurrection" in our generation (the end of this current age of gace). Finally, the Jubilee at the end of the millennial age will defeat death "finally" for all man in "the second resurrection" (Rev. 20) before which all mankind who rejected Christ in this lifetime will be purified in the lake of God's purifying fire.

1 Timothy 4:10 says: *"God is the Savior of **all men**, especially of those who believe."* So, even those who don't believe in this lifetime will eventually come to Christ in future ages as Jesus saves all mankind in the world to come.

Our Father will be faithful to redeem all man and our only decision is **when** we will be a part of it. His sovereignty is above our decisions and He even uses evil to work towards our good if we have set our hearts to love him: *"All things work together for good to them that love God"* (Romans 8:28). Basically, our Father is looking for <a href="31sonshipovercomers.htm" target="_blank">overcomers</a> in this current evil generation who overcome the world through **love** and NOT through control or manipulation. But eventually, all man will be restored through Jesus. This is the law of Jubilee!(2)

Unfortunately this is not what I had been taught for the first 20 years in my walk with Yahweh. The typical evangelical church teaches a hell fire that lasts forever, but this is a mistranslation of key words in Scripture such as the Greek word "olam" which means "age lasting" and not "everlasting" as most English Bible translations use. The purpose of this writing is not to give a full theological discourse of this doctrine, but to simply give testimony of its truth and fruit in my life. The footnotes in this newsletter contain some further research for those who want more Scriptural proof for these things.

Dr. Stephen Jones writes:

> > *In the case of the judicial system itself, how can we expect our judges to establish justice in the courts, prescribing judgments that are neither too lenient nor too harsh, when the Church itself prescribes an infinite and horrible punishment upon all sinners alike, regardless of the nature of their crime? The courts are merely reflecting the values of the people.*
> >
> > *Which is worse, to sentence a man to five years in prison for theft, or to sentence him to a torture chamber for all eternity? Civil judges today know that the punishment should vary, depending on the severity of the crime. Yet much of the Church is still influenced by the Roman logic that the purpose of punishment is to deter crime, rather than to restore justice. With this mindset, it is logical that if punishments are severe enough, law and order will be maintained, and the people will be obedient.*
> >
> > *If they had been students of the divine law, they would have understood that the purpose of judgment is to restore the lawful order by restoring the lost property to the victim, while restoring the sinner to grace and forgiveness.*(1)

The bottom line is that corrective judgment is not to try and scare people into believing so they don't sin anymore, but to restore right relationships!

The lake of fire and river of fire in Scripture is for the purpose of refining, purging, and purifying. Again Stephen Jones writes:

> > *All of our misunderstandings of the lake of fire would easily be solved by a study of God's Law. After all, the law is the most relevant factor in this matter of judgment. Paul says in Romans 6:23, "for the wages of sin is death." Ezekiel 18:20 confirms this: "The person \["soul"\] who sins will die."*
> >
> > *Anyone who studies the divine law will see that death is the worst possible punishment that can be meted out. Even when a man was guilty of multiple murder, the maximum penalty was death. There is no sin worthy of being burned at the stake, much less being burned in a torture chamber for an eternity.*(1)

**Separate** from this fire before the throne, there is a place that Jesus calls "hell" which is **the grave** (*Sheol* in Hebrew and *Gehenna or Hades* in Greek) which was the city dump in biblical times. It was the valley of Hinnom in south Jerusalem where trash and dead animals were burned. Hell is certainly a place of destruction in the natural and shame in the spiritual, but it was NOT a place of eternal torment, since the bible does not use torture as a means of bringing judgment. Hell is a real place that is to be shunned and can be avoided for man only through the saving grace of Jesus Christ, but its not eternal.

The term "eternal" in Scripture means "age lasting" because again, our Father is outside of **time** and works differently throughout the ages ordained for us. Please refer to this link <a href="https://gods-kingdom-ministries.net/teachings/books/creations-jubilee/chapter-4-does-god-punish-endlessly/" target="_blank">Does God Punish Endlessly?</a> to understand that "eternal" and "everlasting" are mistranslations of the Greek and Hebrew words that really mean age-enduring and age-lasting.

It took years for me to understand that God will restore all man. Mainly because here in the bible belt of Alabama we have eternal torment ingrained into us from youth. Back in 2010, I had even written a newsletter coming against this doctrine quoting multiple scriptures and typical evangelical rhetoric to "prove" how it was false; but I was wrong.

Many of the early church fathers taught this truth.(1) And there is much good teaching on the Salvation of All Mankind below.(2)

Eventually every knee will bow and every tongue confess Jesus is LORD because they will see His goodness!

***"God our Saviour ... will have all men to be saved."
1 Timothy 2:3-4
****KJV*
